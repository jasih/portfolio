Information About Me

Hi, I'm Janet. I graduated from the University of Memphis in December of 2021 with a bachelors in Computer Science. I originally started out as an art major, wanting to do graphic design, and being inspired by the anime made from studio ghibli and some games that I've played with my family, I've always wanted to take my artwork to the next level by creating a game with my own artwork, or create artwork in that studio ghibli style. So, I transitioned into computer science after one of my cousins had convinced me that it would be a great opportunity for me since there weren't many women in this field. And, after I took him up on that offer, I joined his club called the Association for Computing Machinery or the ACM for short. While I was there, I met many great developers who helped me learn Java concepts such as OOP and helped me know the fundamentals of other languages like Python and the C languages as well. And throughout my time working with them and also other developers from my courses, I also learned how to use tools and frameworks such as Eclipse, Visual Studio Code and Unity. And during the last few months of my senior year, I realized my love for software development. Later on, I became the student chair of the ACM and even decided to create my own personal projects such as making my own Discord bot using Javascript. And since then, I've been looking to continue to expand my knowledge and improve my skills which led me to the FastTrack program at Cook Systems.
